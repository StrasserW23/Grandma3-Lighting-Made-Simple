Phasers

In grandMA3, a phaser is a dynamic tool that generates movement, color chases, and automated repeating effects by cycling between two or more steps. Unlike static presets, which consist of a single step, phasers run through multi-step sequences, giving you control over parameters such as speed, phase, width, and transitions to create intricate, evolving lighting designs.

Effects

  • Fly-Outs: A fly-out is when lights move quickly from one position to another — usually outward or off a focal point. What it looks like on stage: Beams start centered on an artist Then suddenly “fly” outward across the stage or audience Creates energy and expansion How it’s used in grandMA3: You create this using: Position presets A movement effect (often a one-shot or short effect) Fast transition timing It’s common in big musical hits or drops — think stadium rock moments.
  • Figure 8s:A movement pattern where a moving head traces the shape of the number 8. What it looks like on stage: Smooth looping motion Dynamic but not chaotic Great for songs that need flow How it works in grandMA3: You use a position effect with:X and Y phase offset Sine wave shapes Adjusted width and speed Because grandMA3 uses a powerful effects engine, you can control: Size of the 8 Speed Phase between fixtures. This is common in worship environments (like what you do at Rivers Crossing) or mid-tempo songs.
  • Circles: A movement pattern where lights move in a circular motion. What it looks like on stage: Smooth orbiting beams Can be tight and subtle or wide and dramatic How you program it? In grandMA3:Use a position effect Apply sine wave on X and cosine on Y Adjust phase to spread fixtures evenlyCircles are cleaner and more predictable than figure 8s.
    • Chase: A chase is when lights turn on and off in sequence. What it looks like: Lights “run” across the stage Creates rhythm and movement Think classic rock or EDM builds In grandMA3: You create a chase using: Step-based sequences Cue timing Or a dimmer effect with phase spread You can chase: Dimmer Color Position Beam effects Chases are heavily timing-based — very musical.
  • Dimus: I’m assuming you meant Dimmer or Dimmer effects. What it is: Controls the brightness of fixtures. What it looks like: Pulsing Flashing Breathing effects Strobes In grandMA3: Use: Dimmer effects Waveforms (sine, square, ramp, etc.) Speed master control

    • Dimmer effects are some of the most powerful tools for matching music energy — especially in high-impact songs.

    Gobos

    In grandMA3 you don’t physically insert gobos — you control them digitally by adjusting fixture parameters:

    A gobo is a small, stenciled, or etched metal or glass disc placed inside a lighting fixture to project patterns, images, or logos onto surfaces.

    Standing for "Goes Between Optics" (or "Go Before Optics"), it acts as a template to shape light for theatrical, corporate, or event design.

  • Selection – Chooses which pattern is active
  • Gobo Rotation – Spins the pattern
  • Gobo Indexing – Locks the gobo at a precise angle
  • Gobo Shake – Adds a vibration/shimmer effect
  • Gobo Morph – Transitions between gobos (on some fixtures)

    • These controls live in the fixture’s attribute encoder pages (usually under Beam).
